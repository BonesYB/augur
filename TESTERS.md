# Trying out Augur

Thanks for taking Augur for a spin! It's an early prototype — a planning tool for
running tabletop RPG sessions, laid out a bit like a video editor's timeline. What I'm
really trying to find out is whether that "timeline" idea feels natural to people who
*don't* edit video. So honest reactions are the whole point — if something's confusing,
that's useful information, not you doing it wrong.

---

## Installing it

It's not code-signed yet (that costs money I haven't spent on a hobby project), so both
Windows and Mac will throw a scary-looking warning the first time. The app is safe — the
warning just means "this developer hasn't paid for a certificate." Here's how to get past it.

### Windows
1. Run **Augur_0.1.0_x64-setup.exe**.
2. If you see **"Windows protected your PC"**, click **More info → Run anyway**.
3. Launch Augur from the Start menu.

### Mac
1. Open **Augur_0.1.0_universal.dmg** and drag **Augur** into your Applications folder.
2. macOS will probably refuse to open it — saying it's "damaged" or from an
   "unidentified developer." It is *not* damaged. To clear the warning:
   - Open the **Terminal** app (Cmd-Space, type "Terminal").
   - Paste this line and press Enter:
     ```
     xattr -cr /Applications/Augur.app
     ```
   - Now open Augur normally (double-click, or right-click → Open).

## What to try

When it opens you'll land on a built-in **guided tour** — a timeline of nine lessons.
You don't need to install anything else or open any files; it's all right there.

1. Click each block, **1 through 9**, and read what appears in the big middle panel.
2. Drag the blue **playhead** (the vertical line) left-to-right across the timeline.
3. Drag blocks to reorder them, drag their edges to resize, and right-click one.
4. Generally poke at everything. Try to break it.

(If you get curious and want to load a real mini-adventure, ask me for the
`tutorial.augur` file and use the **⤒ .augur** button.)
